# 📱 Responsible Social Media Use – Checklist for College Students

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it. |
| 2 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** – Regularly review who can see your posts, profile, and personal details on every platform. |
| 3 | **Give credit when sharing others' work** – Always tag or mention the original creator when reposting photos, writing, art, or videos. |
| 4 | **Be respectful and constructive in comments** – Engage in healthy debates with facts and polite language, even when you disagree strongly. |
| 5 | **Report and block harmful content/accounts** – Use platform tools to report harassment, misinformation, or abusive accounts to keep your space safe. |
| 6 | **Verify information before sharing** – Cross-check news or claims on trusted sources (e.g., PIB Fact Check, Reuters) before hitting "Share" or "Forward." |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily routine where strangers can see it. |
| 2 | **Don't post in anger or under peer pressure** – Emotional or impulsive posts can damage your reputation permanently; screenshots last forever. |
| 3 | **Don't engage with or spread cyberbullying** – Liking, sharing, or staying silent about bullying content makes you part of the problem. Report it instead. |
| 4 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers blindly** – Fake profiles are used for scams and phishing; verify before connecting. |
| 5 | **Don't plagiarise or impersonate others** – Never create fake profiles of real people or post someone else's content as your own. |
| 6 | **Don't overshare your location in real time** – Posting live location or "checking in" at every spot tells bad actors exactly where you are. |
