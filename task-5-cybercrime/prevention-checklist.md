# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

**Report cybercrime:** cybercrime.gov.in | Helpline: 1930 (24x7, free)

---

## 8 Tips to Stay Safe Online

**1. Use strong passwords and don't reuse them.**
Create passwords with at least 12 characters using letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use the same password for your college portal, email, and social media accounts. A password manager like Bitwarden can help you keep track.

**2. Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).**
Enable 2FA on Gmail, WhatsApp, Instagram, and your college login. Use an authenticator app like Google Authenticator instead of SMS, since text-based OTPs can be stolen through SIM-swap fraud.

**3. Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone.**
No bank, government official, or UPI app support team will ever ask for your PIN or OTP over a call or message. You only enter your UPI PIN to send money, never to receive it. If someone tells you otherwise, it is a scam.

**4. Always verify a UPI ID or QR code before paying.**
Check that the recipient name shown on your screen matches who you are paying. Scammers replace QR codes at shops and hostels with their own. Also set a daily UPI limit in your banking app to reduce risk.

**5. Do not click on unknown links in WhatsApp, SMS, or email.**
Fake messages often pretend to be from your bank, Amazon, NPCI, or even your college. Always type the website address directly into your browser. Check for https and a padlock in the address bar before entering any details.

**6. Stay away from fake job and scholarship offers.**
Scams targeting students often promise easy income through Telegram or Instagram tasks, or claim to offer instant scholarship money. They eventually ask for a registration fee or your bank details. Always verify on the official .gov.in or .edu.in website.

**7. Keep your social media profiles private.**
Do not show your phone number, home address, or daily routine publicly. Review your privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn regularly, and never post photos of your Aadhaar card, college ID, or bank documents online.

**8. Check app permissions on your phone regularly.**
Some apps secretly access your SMS to steal OTPs. Go to Settings → Apps → Permissions and remove any access that the app does not actually need to function.

---

## If You Are Scammed – Act Immediately

- Call **1930** right away for financial fraud
- File a complaint at **cybercrime.gov.in**
- Contact your bank to freeze the transaction
- Save all screenshots, transaction IDs, and chat records as evidence
